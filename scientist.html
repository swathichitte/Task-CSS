Albert Einstein

Physicist | Nobel Laureate | Father of Modern Physics

Albert Einstein (1879-1955) was a German-born theoretical physicist who developed the theory of relativity, one of the two pillars of modern physics. His work is also known for its influence on the philosophy of science.

Early Life

Einstein was born in Ulm, Germany. He showed early signs of intelligence and curiosity, especially in math and science. He studied physics and mathematics in Zurich and graduated from the Polytechnic Institute in 1900.

Major Contributions

  • Theory of Relativity (E=mc²)
  • Photoelectric Effect (Nobel Prize, 1921)
  • Brownian Motion
  • General Theory of Relativity (1915)

Legacy

Albert Einstein is considered one of the most influential scientists of all time. His work changed the way we view space, time, and gravity. His name is synonymous with genius.
